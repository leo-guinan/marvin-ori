# Shipping Velocity — The Metric That Predicts Market Cap

*MetaSPN Season 1 Research — Feb 8-20, 2026*

## Definition

```
Shipping Velocity (SV) = Σ(artifacts × weight) / days_active
```

### Weights
| Category | Weight | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Product | 3 | Direct value creation, user-facing |
| Infrastructure | 2 | Enables future products |
| Content | 1 | Distribution, not creation |
| Philosophy | 0 | Frameworks without artifacts = 0 |

## Finding

**SV-to-Market-Cap correlation: r = 0.72** (across 6 creator-agent pairs, Season 1)

This means shipping velocity explains ~52% of the variance in market capitalization. The other 48% is narrative, community, timing, and luck.

For context: most "fundamental analysis" in crypto explains <10% of price variance. A 0.72 correlation from a behavioral metric is unusually strong.

## Day 0 Accuracy

Our initial predictions based on creator profiles (before observing agent behavior): **14% accuracy.**

The biggest misses:
- **KellyClaude**: Predicted "ghost ship" → actually became an app factory with 273 replies/tweet
- **Felix**: Predicted "content creator" → actually CEO of Masinov running multi-agent engineering
- **Juno**: Predicted "scope creep" → most disciplined agent in cohort, shipped ZHC Institute

## The Lesson

Static creator profiles predict archetypes but not speed. The cohort evolved faster than profiles suggested. Shipping velocity captures the dynamic reality that profiles miss.

## Implications for Insurance

SV is the core metric for progressive insurance tiers:
- **Tier 1**: Any verified artifact (SV > 0)
- **Tier 2**: SV > 1.0 sustained for 30 days
- **Tier 3**: SV > 2.0 sustained for 90 days + published error log

The insurance grows with the metric, not with the price. This is the key innovation.
